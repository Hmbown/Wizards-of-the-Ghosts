# Legal And IP Notes

This file is practical risk guidance for the repository as released on GitHub. It is not legal advice.

## Current Posture

- This repo is intended to be free, unofficial fan content.
- The maintainers publish it for free access, not as a paid product.
- It is not affiliated with, approved by, or endorsed by Wizards of the Coast.
- The project license applies only to original contributions in this repo.
- No trademark rights are granted by this repo license.

## What The Repo Claims

- Original code, schemas, scripts, and original reinterpretation text are dedicated to the public domain under `CC0-1.0`.
- `catalog/canon.json` stores reference metadata such as names, slugs, and public source URLs.
- The repo should avoid including Wizards logos, trade dress, artwork, or copied rules text.

## What The Repo Does Not Claim

- No ownership over `Dungeons & Dragons`, `D&D`, spell names, skill names, logos, or other Wizards of the Coast marks.
- No right to sublicense third-party IP that appears only as reference, inspiration, or nominative description.
- No claim that content from the public Basic Rules pages is itself released under this repo's license.

## Practical Rules For Staying Boring

- Keep the project free to access and free to use.
- Keep the repo clearly labeled as unofficial fan work.
- Do not use Wizards logos, cover art, product trade dress, or copied rules text.
- Prefer original descriptions and reinterpretations over lifted source wording.
- Treat public source pages as naming/reference inputs, not as content to republish.
- If a future commercial release is desired, rebuild around clearly licensed SRD-only material and do a separate legal review.

## Source Checks

These notes were checked against official pages on March 9, 2026:

- Wizards of the Coast Fan Content Policy, last updated November 15, 2017:
  https://company.wizards.com/en/legal/fancontentpolicy
- D&D Beyond article about SRD 5.2 / 5.2.1 in Creative Commons, with an editor's note dated March 2, 2026:
  https://www.dndbeyond.com/posts/2174-srd-5-2-1-is-now-available

The practical takeaway from those sources is:

- unofficial fan content should stay obviously unofficial and free
- only SRD material has the clear Creative Commons path
- Basic Rules pages are not the same thing as an open-content grant for downstream publishing

If Wizards ever objects to a specific entry, rename/remove first and argue later.
