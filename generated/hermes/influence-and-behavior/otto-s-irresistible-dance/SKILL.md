# Otto’s Irresistible Dance
Make something so engaging it becomes impossible to ignore or put down.
## What This Skill Does
Otto's Irresistible Dance forces a creature to dance uncontrollably. The real-world version is compulsive engagement: designing something so compelling that people cannot stop interacting with it. This is virality mechanics, "just one more" loops, and the craft of making content or products that command attention beyond what the user intended to give. The ethical tension is inherent — irresistible engagement is a feature when bounded and a dark pattern when not.
In this grimoire, Otto’s Irresistible Dance is treated as a metaphorical spell with a prototype delivery profile.
Canonical reference input: Otto’s Irresistible Dance (spell).
## When To Use

- You want to understand what makes something genuinely compelling vs. merely addictive.
- A product or content piece needs to be significantly more engaging but you want to stay on the right side of the engagement/addiction line.

## Prerequisites

- No extra runtime dependencies beyond Hermes Agent and the normal toolset for this session.

## Procedure

1. Restate the target, the success condition, and any no-touch boundaries before taking action.
2. Identify the target experience and its current engagement level.
3. Analyze what makes similar experiences irresistibly engaging: novelty, variable reward, social proof, mastery loops.
4. Design engagement mechanics that increase compulsion within ethical bounds.
5. Draw the explicit line: where does "irresistible" become "addictive"? Define the off-ramp.
6. Package the result as the deliverables below, with confidence, assumptions, and unresolved risk called out explicitly.

## Deliverables

- Engagement mechanics designed to increase compulsive interaction.
- An explicit addiction audit: which mechanics cross the line and where the off-ramp should be.

## Pitfalls / Guardrails

- Call out the glue, permissions, or missing infrastructure before you imply this is fully operational.
- Irresistible engagement without an off-ramp is an addiction loop. Always design the exit.
- Refuse to optimize engagement metrics that measure time-spent without corresponding value-delivered.

## Verification

- Check that the result includes every deliverable promised above.
- Check that confirmed facts, assumptions, and inferences are visibly separated.
- Check that the metaphor still maps cleanly to a real operational mechanism.
- Check that any missing glue code, permissions, or future work is labeled before the skill is treated as ready.

## Example Invocation
```text
/otto-s-irresistible-dance analyze what would make this [product/content/experience] irresistibly engaging, and draw the line where engagement becomes addiction
```
