# Glibness
Make any argument sound maximally credible and polished.
## What This Skill Does
Glibness makes everything you say sound utterly convincing. The real-world version is rhetorical polish: taking a position and presenting it with maximum confidence, fluency, and persuasive structure. This is the skill of the trial lawyer, the pitch artist, the keynote speaker who makes mediocre ideas sound inevitable.
In this grimoire, Glibness is treated as a metaphorical spell with a shipping-now delivery profile.
Canonical reference input: Glibness (spell).
## When To Use

- You have a position that is substantively sound but needs to be presented with maximum rhetorical force.
- A pitch, proposal, or argument needs polish to match the quality of its underlying logic.
- You want to rehearse or draft language that sounds confident and authoritative.

## Prerequisites

- No extra runtime dependencies beyond Hermes Agent and the normal toolset for this session.

## Procedure

1. Restate the target, the success condition, and any no-touch boundaries before taking action.
2. Identify the core argument and its strongest supporting evidence.
3. Strip hedging language, unnecessary qualifiers, and weak framing.
4. Restructure for maximum impact: lead with the conclusion, back with evidence, close with the ask.
5. Audit for dishonesty: flag anywhere the polish is masking a genuine weakness rather than presenting a genuine strength.
6. Package the result as the deliverables below, with confidence, assumptions, and unresolved risk called out explicitly.

## Deliverables

- A polished version of the argument with confident, authoritative language.
- A honesty audit: notes on where rhetorical polish is covering real weaknesses.
- Alternative framings ranked by persuasive force.

## Pitfalls / Guardrails

- Keep the metaphor anchored to a real mechanism instead of drifting into lore.
- Flag when polish is compensating for a fundamentally flawed argument. Glibness should amplify truth, not manufacture it.
- Distinguish between legitimate confidence and dishonest certainty. Saying something confidently does not make it true.

## Verification

- Check that the result includes every deliverable promised above.
- Check that confirmed facts, assumptions, and inferences are visibly separated.
- Check that the metaphor still maps cleanly to a real operational mechanism.

## Example Invocation
```text
/glibness make this [pitch/proposal/argument] sound as credible and polished as possible, and flag anywhere the polish is hiding a real weakness
```
