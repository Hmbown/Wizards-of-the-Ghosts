# Performance
Stage technical substance so an audience can see why it matters.
## What This Skill Does
Use this skill to turn dry technical material into a polished demo, narrative walkthrough, or audience-aware presentation without dumbing it down. It focuses on pacing, reveal order, and memorable framing so the work lands in the room.
In this grimoire, Performance is treated as a metaphorical skill with a shipping-now delivery profile.
Canonical reference input: Performance (skill).
## When To Use

- You are turning docs, metrics, or a prototype into a demo, talk, launch note, or README.
- The content is sound, but the audience needs a stronger narrative arc to stay with it.
- You need polish, pacing, and audience energy rather than more raw detail.

## Prerequisites

- No extra runtime dependencies beyond Hermes Agent and the normal toolset for this session.

## Procedure

1. Restate the target, the success condition, and any no-touch boundaries before taking action.
2. Identify the audience, medium, and the moment that should land hardest.
3. Structure the material into a clear arc with reveals, evidence, and transitions that keep attention.
4. Return a presentation-ready draft plus speaker, demo, or pacing notes.
5. Package the result as the deliverables below, with confidence, assumptions, and unresolved risk called out explicitly.

## Deliverables

- A polished presentation, demo script, or narrative draft.
- A section order or story arc for the material.
- Notes on pacing, visuals, and likely audience questions.

## Pitfalls / Guardrails

- Keep the metaphor anchored to a real mechanism instead of drifting into lore.
- Do not hide critical caveats or uncertainty for the sake of applause.
- Keep polish in service of comprehension, not hype detached from evidence.

## Verification

- Check that the result includes every deliverable promised above.
- Check that confirmed facts, assumptions, and inferences are visibly separated.
- Check that the metaphor still maps cleanly to a real operational mechanism.

## Example Invocation
```text
/performance turn this technical material into a polished presentation that will land with the intended audience
```
