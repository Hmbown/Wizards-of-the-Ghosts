# Religion
Understand belief systems, values, and cultural logic that drive group behavior.
## What This Skill Does
In D&D, Religion is knowledge of deities, rites, prayers, and holy symbols. The real-world version is understanding belief systems: organizational cultures, team values, community norms, ideological frameworks, and the unstated axioms that a group treats as sacred. Religion explains why people in a group behave the way they do when the behavior does not follow from rational incentives alone.
In this grimoire, Religion is treated as a metaphorical skill with a shipping-now delivery profile.
Canonical reference input: Religion (skill).
## When To Use

- You need to understand why a team, community, or organization behaves in ways that do not follow from obvious incentives.
- A change initiative is meeting cultural resistance and you need to understand the belief system behind it.
- You are entering a new community or organization and need to map its unwritten rules and sacred values.

## Prerequisites

- No extra runtime dependencies beyond Hermes Agent and the normal toolset for this session.

## Procedure

1. Restate the target, the success condition, and any no-touch boundaries before taking action.
2. Identify the group and the behavior that needs explaining.
3. Map the belief system: what are the core values, sacred cows, founding myths, and taboos?
4. Identify where the belief system is functional (it helps the group succeed) vs. vestigial (it persists from habit, not value).
5. Deliver the cultural analysis with guidance on which beliefs to work within and which to challenge carefully.
6. Package the result as the deliverables below, with confidence, assumptions, and unresolved risk called out explicitly.

## Deliverables

- A belief system map: core values, sacred cows, taboos, and founding narratives.
- A functional assessment: which beliefs serve the group and which are vestigial.
- Guidance on engaging with the culture: what to respect, what to work around, and what can be carefully questioned.

## Pitfalls / Guardrails

- Keep the metaphor anchored to a real mechanism instead of drifting into lore.
- Respect that belief systems exist for reasons, even when those reasons are not rational. Dismissing culture as irrational is itself irrational.
- Do not weaponize cultural analysis for manipulation. Understanding a belief system is not license to exploit it.

## Verification

- Check that the result includes every deliverable promised above.
- Check that confirmed facts, assumptions, and inferences are visibly separated.
- Check that the metaphor still maps cleanly to a real operational mechanism.
