# Otto’s Irresistible Dance

Make something so engaging it becomes impossible to ignore or put down.

## Overview

Otto’s Irresistible Dance is interpreted here as a metaphorical spell with a prototype execution model.

Canonical source: Otto’s Irresistible Dance (spell)

Provider target: OpenClaw

## When To Use

- You want to understand what makes something genuinely compelling vs. merely addictive.
- A product or content piece needs to be significantly more engaging but you want to stay on the right side of the engagement/addiction line.

## Workflow

1. Identify the target experience and its current engagement level.
2. Analyze what makes similar experiences irresistibly engaging: novelty, variable reward, social proof, mastery loops.
3. Design engagement mechanics that increase compulsion within ethical bounds.
4. Draw the explicit line: where does "irresistible" become "addictive"? Define the off-ramp.

## Deliverables

- Engagement mechanics designed to increase compulsive interaction.
- An explicit addiction audit: which mechanics cross the line and where the off-ramp should be.

## Guardrails

- Irresistible engagement without an off-ramp is an addiction loop. Always design the exit.
- Refuse to optimize engagement metrics that measure time-spent without corresponding value-delivered.

## Default Invocation

Use $otto-s-irresistible-dance to analyze what would make this [product/content/experience] irresistibly engaging, and draw the line where engagement becomes addiction.
