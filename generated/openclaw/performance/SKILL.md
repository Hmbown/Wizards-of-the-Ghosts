# Performance

Stage technical substance so an audience can see why it matters.

## Overview

Performance is interpreted here as a metaphorical skill with a shipping-now execution model.

Canonical source: Performance (skill)

Provider target: OpenClaw

## When To Use

- You are turning docs, metrics, or a prototype into a demo, talk, launch note, or README.
- The content is sound, but the audience needs a stronger narrative arc to stay with it.
- You need polish, pacing, and audience energy rather than more raw detail.

## Workflow

1. Identify the audience, medium, and the moment that should land hardest.
2. Structure the material into a clear arc with reveals, evidence, and transitions that keep attention.
3. Return a presentation-ready draft plus speaker, demo, or pacing notes.

## Deliverables

- A polished presentation, demo script, or narrative draft.
- A section order or story arc for the material.
- Notes on pacing, visuals, and likely audience questions.

## Guardrails

- Do not hide critical caveats or uncertainty for the sake of applause.
- Keep polish in service of comprehension, not hype detached from evidence.
