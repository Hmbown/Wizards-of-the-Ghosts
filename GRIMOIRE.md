# Grimoire

This is the GitHub-browseable index for the spellbook.

If you want the actual generated skill files, run `npm run build:skills` and inspect `generated/openclaw/` or `generated/hermes/` locally. If you want the fastest way to understand the project on GitHub, start here.

The Hermes packaging uses 8 operational categories designed for agent discovery. The shelves below are curated highlights from each.

## Quick-Start Shelf

1. `Detect Magic` (`detect-magic`) [shipping-now, metaphorical]: Scan a repo, workflow, or system for the real agent, tool, and automation surfaces before acting.
2. `Identify` (`identify`) [shipping-now, metaphorical]: Explain what a file, service, artifact, or workflow actually does.
3. `Comprehend Languages` (`comprehend-languages`) [shipping-now, metaphorical]: Translate code, jargon, protocols, or human language into operational meaning.
4. `Mage Hand` (`mage-hand`) [shipping-now, hybrid]: Make small, careful software manipulations without swinging a battleaxe at the whole system.
5. `Glyph of Warding` (`glyph-of-warding`) [shipping-now, literal]: Turn watch conditions into real monitors, alerts, or policy triggers.
6. `Foresight` (`foresight`) [shipping-now, metaphorical]: Forecast likely outcomes before committing to a change, launch, or migration.
7. `Dream` (`dream`) [shipping-now, metaphorical]: Deliver the right briefing before the recipient wakes up needing it.
8. `Dancing Lights` (`dancing-lights`) [shipping-now, hybrid]: Hang small status signals in the dark so humans can orient quickly.
9. `Scrying` (`scrying`) [prototype, literal]: Observe a remote system or environment in near real time.
10. `Knock` (`knock`) [prototype, literal]: Get through access friction via legitimate unlock and recovery paths.

## Grounded Circles

These spells are the ones that currently declare real integration requirements instead of floating entirely on metaphor.

- `Home Assistant` circle: `Awaken`, `Dancing Lights`, `Glyph of Warding`, `Light`, `Scrying`, `Sleep`
- `Slack` circle: `Dream`, `Magic Mouth`, `Message`, `Sending`, `Silence`

In practice that means the spell only really wakes up once the required environment variables and system tools are present.

## The Spellbook Refuses These

The public Hermes and OpenClaw surfaces intentionally do not ship:

- `Compulsion`
- `Dominate Monster`
- `Dominate Person`
- `Geas`
- `Modify Memory`

The point of the spellbook is not to smuggle coercion into a wizard hat.

## Reading the Sigils

- `shipping-now`: This can honestly work with current agents, tools, and integrations.
- `prototype`: The idea is plausible now, but it still needs glue code, permissions, or setup discipline.
- `speculative`: Interesting enough to keep, not honest enough to oversell.
- `metaphorical`: The skill keeps the shape of the spell more than the literal effect.
- `hybrid`: The spell keeps one foot in theater and one in practical workflow.
- `literal`: The spell tries to cause a directly analogous real-world effect.
